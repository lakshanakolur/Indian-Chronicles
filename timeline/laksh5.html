Non-Cooperation Movement

10 March 1922


The Non-Cooperation Movement was pitched in under leadership of Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress from September 1920 to February 1922, marking a new awakening in the Indian Independence Movement. After a series of traumatic events, Gandhiji realised that there was no prospect of getting any fair treatment at the hands of British, so he planned to withdraw the nation's co-operation, thus launching the Non-Cooperation Movement and thereby marring the British administrative set up.
